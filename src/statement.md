# Artist Statement

My practice begins with looking — patient, unhurried, often the same
view across many days. Each piece is an attempt to hold a fleeting
quality of light or attention long enough that another person can
also see it.

I'm drawn to the threshold moments: a window before the sun reaches it,
a hand at rest, a kitchen between meals. The work is small and slow on
purpose. I want it to ask for the kind of attention it was made with.
